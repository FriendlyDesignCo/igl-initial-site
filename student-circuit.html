Student Circuit

Overview

The Student Gaming Circuit is the first-ever educational fundraising competitive gaming tournament; it will be hosted by the Independent Gaming League and organized by students of De La Salle High School. Chess teams, as well as their non-chess-playing classmates, from various high schools and middle schools will compete head-to-head in a selection of free tournaments against students from rival schools. The result of each tournament will be recorded, and the school with the most combined points will walk away as the area champion and receive a financial contribution from IGL.

Chess Tournament

Each school can register 1 chess team of up to 6 players. Registering a chess team is free and chess team members are eligible for a 50% discount on a two-day festival pass which is eligible for product giveaways, cash prizes and much more. The tournament structure and rules will be determined and enforced by De La Salle High School. The winning chess team will receive a financial contribution to help fund their school's technology and gaming programs. For more info on registering a school chess team, fill out this form: rndnerds.com/NGFchess

Video Games Points Race

Students who are not part of the chess team can attend NOLA Gaming Fest at a discounted rate of $25 ($5 off a regular ticket), and they can participate in the student-only tournaments and earn points for their school. Use discount code 'student2016' at check out. You will be required to present a valid student ID at registration to enter otherwise a full price ticket will be required.

At the end of NOLA Gaming Fest, the school with the most points earned will receive a financial contribution, with individual winners also receiving prizes.

The full schedule of student-only tournaments is coming soon.
